Marie Curie

The woman who changed the face of science

Dr. Norman Borlaug,Third from the left trains biologist in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here’s a timeline of Marie Curie’s life:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Poland
  • 1891 - Moves to Paris to study physics and mathematics at the Sorbonne
  • 1895 - Marries physicist Pierre Curie
  • 1898 - Discovers the elements polonium and radium
  • 1903 - Wins Nobel Prize in Physics (shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel)
  • 1911 - Wins second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry
  • 1914 - Helps establish the Radium Institute in Paris
  • 1914-1918 - Develops mobile radiography units during World War I
  • 1934 - Dies from aplastic anemia caused by prolonged exposure to radiation
